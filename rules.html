Dimensions ClubCode The Web Competition

Web Development Competition

Objective:

Redesign the Texas Wesleyan Computer Science website. Gain valuable experience that will help you move up in the future.
  • Keep existing site content(links etc)
  • Create a website with the target audience in mind: Current students & faculty of the CS department
  • Interactive and mobile friendly website
  • Organized and easy to use
  • Contact page
    • A way to subscribe or unsubscribe from the mailing list
    • Social Media links
    • What else would you as a student would like to see there?

Rules:

  • Don't use Bootstrap (or any other) themes
  • Don’t use website builders
    • Wix, Wordpress, Tumblr, etc
  • No malicious content
  • Don't plagiarize

Deadlines:

Rough draft / Mock-up : December 20th
Final Submission: February 20th

Such a long period of time is given to students with understanding that some might not have prior experience in web development, but would like to learn and participate. This is also an amazing opportunity for resume building!

FAQ | Common Questions

Where do I submit my work?

Email vslysenko@txwes.edu or eaayala@txwes.edu (subject to change)

Who will be judging the competition?

  • The final code will be judged by Y. Zhang and S. Rodriguez.
  • The mock up will not be judged, as it is purely for your own experience in selling websites.

What is a mock-up?
  • A mock-up is a visual way of representing a website (website’s pages). Depending on the level of details, a mock-up can have only some or all visual properties of a final solution.
  • It is simply an image (or PDF).
  • Free Tools: Mockup Tools
  • The mock-up will not be judged, as it is purely for your own experience in selling websites.
Can we work in groups for this competition?

Yes. The prize money will be split in that case. Include all names in the submission.
